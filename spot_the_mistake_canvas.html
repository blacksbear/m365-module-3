The Reply All trap ✓

Something is wrong with how this email is being sent.

Outlook — New Message
Reply
Reply All ←
Forward
Delete
To:HR Team + All Staff (47 people)
From:Jordan M.
RE: Confidential — Accommodation Request
"Thanks for letting us know. I think we should also consider..."
The mistake

Reply All sends a confidential HR message to all 47 staff members. The original email was only meant for HR and the employee's manager — this is a serious privacy breach.

The fix

Always use Reply — not Reply All — unless every single recipient genuinely needs your response. Before sending, double-check the To field. When in doubt about a sensitive email, reply only to the sender.

Top 10 Finance Tips This Week

URGENT: Board deck needs changes
HR Team
Submit timesheets by Friday
⚠ No folders created — all 547 emails in Inbox
The mistake

No folder structure after months on the job. Urgent client and manager emails are buried under newsletters and automated notifications — critical messages can be missed entirely.

The fix

Set up folders in your first week: Clients, HR, Projects, Meetings. Use Inbox Rules to automatically route recurring senders (e.g. newsletters) so your inbox only holds what actually needs your attention.

Ignored invitations ✓

Something is wrong with these meeting invitations.

Outlook — Calendar
Pending invitations
Q2 Planning Session
Mon Mar 9 · 2:00 PM · Sarah Chen
No response ←
Client Call — Apex Financial
Tue Mar 10 · 11:00 AM · Michael T.
No response
Weekly Team Standup
Wed Mar 11 · 9:00 AM · Team Lead
No response
The mistake

Three meeting invitations from last week have received no response. Meeting organizers cannot finalise headcounts, book rooms, or plan agendas without knowing whether you're attending.

The fix

Always respond within 24 hours — Accept, Tentative, or Decline. Even declining is helpful. Add a short message when declining so your colleagues can plan around your absence. Use "Propose New Time" if you're available at a different slot.

The missing attachment ✓

To:Director of Finance
Q1 Financial Summary — Board Review
"Hi Director, please see the attached Q1 summary report ← for your review ahead of Friday's board meeting. Let me know if you have any questions."
!
No files attached
The mistake

The email mentions "attached report" but no file was actually attached. The Director now has to follow up to get the document — wasting valuable time before an important board meeting.

The fix

Outlook shows an automatic warning pop-up if you type "attach" or "attached" but haven't added a file — always pay attention to it. Even better: attach the file first, then write the email body, so you can't forget.
